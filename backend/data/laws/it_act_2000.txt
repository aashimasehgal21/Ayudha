Information Technology Act, 2000 — Key Points (Women & Safety Focus)

1. Section 66A (struck down in 2015) — sending offensive messages (no longer valid).
2. Section 66C — identity theft (using photos, impersonation, misuse of accounts).
3. Section 66D — cheating by impersonation using computer resources.
4. Section 67 — publishing or transmitting obscene material online.
5. Section 67A — sexually explicit content (revenge porn), punishable with imprisonment.
6. Section 67B — child sexual content or child pornography.
7. Useful for cyberstalking, blackmail, online harassment, privacy violations.
