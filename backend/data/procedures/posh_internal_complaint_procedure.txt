POSH Internal Complaint Committee (ICC) Complaint Procedure
-----------------------------------------------------------

The procedure for reporting workplace sexual harassment under the 
Sexual Harassment of Women at Workplace (Prevention, Prohibition and Redressal) Act, 2013 (POSH Act).

1. Eligibility
   - Any woman employee (permanent, temporary, intern, consultant, visitor).
   - Complaint must relate to sexual harassment at the workplace.

2. Time Limit
   - Complaint must be filed within 3 months of the incident.
   - ICC may extend the limit by an additional 3 months if justified.

3. How to File the Complaint
   - Submit a written complaint to the Internal Complaints Committee (ICC).
   - Complaint must include:
       • Description of the incident(s)
       • Date, time, and location
       • Names of witnesses (if any)
   - Email or physical copy both accepted.

4. Assistance for Complaint Writing
   - If the woman is unable to write, ICC must provide assistance.
   - A friend, coworker, or legal representative may help submit the complaint.

5. Steps After Complaint Submission
   - ICC acknowledges the complaint.
   - Within 7 days, ICC sends a copy to the respondent (accused).

6. Conciliation Option (Optional)
   - Complainant may request conciliation.
   - Monetary settlement is NOT allowed.
   - If conciliation succeeds, ICC closes the case.

7. Formal Inquiry (If no conciliation)
   - ICC conducts a fair inquiry similar to a workplace disciplinary process.
   - Both parties can present evidence, witnesses, and documents.
   - Inquiry must be completed within 90 days.

8. Interim Reliefs (During the inquiry)
   - Transfer of complainant or respondent  
   - Paid leave for complainant up to 3 months  
   - Any other necessary relief  

9. Final Report
   - ICC submits a written report within 10 days to:
       • Employer  
       • Both parties  
   - Report must recommend:
       • Punishment OR  
       • Closure of complaint if not proven  

10. Punishments (If harassment is proven)
   - Written apology  
   - Warning or reprimand  
   - Termination  
   - Deduction from salary as compensation  
   - Mandated counselling  

11. Appeal
   - Either party may appeal within 90 days of the ICC decision.
