Cybercrime Reporting Procedure (Women Safety)
---------------------------------------------

1. What is considered a cybercrime?
   - Online stalking or harassment
   - Morphing, editing, or sharing private images
   - Cyber bullying, threats, blackmail
   - Impersonation on social media
   - Non-consensual pornography (revenge porn)

2. Where to Report
   - National Cyber Crime Portal: https://cybercrime.gov.in
   - Cyber Helpline Number: 1930
   - Local Police Station / Cyber Cell
   - Women's Safety Cyber Cell (State-specific)

3. How to File a Complaint Online
   - Visit: https://cybercrime.gov.in
   - Click on “Report Women/Child Related Crime”
   - Fill details:
       • Name, mobile number, email  
       • Description of the incident  
       • Upload screenshots, links, chats  
   - Submit the form

4. What Evidence to Collect
   - Screenshots of messages, posts, emails
   - URLs of profiles
   - Phone numbers or email IDs of perpetrators
   - Chat logs and timestamps

5. FIR Registration
   - Cyber offences are cognizable
   - FIR MUST be registered without delay
   - Police cannot refuse filing in jurisdiction issues
     (IT Act allows nationwide jurisdiction)

6. Protection for the Victim
   - Identity confidentiality
   - No device seizure without acknowledgement
   - Home recording option for statements

7. Investigation Timeline
   - Evidence preservation request accepted within 72 hours
   - Digital forensic analysis is initiated
