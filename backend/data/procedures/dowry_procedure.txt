Dowry Harassment & Dowry Complaint Procedure  
(Under Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 & IPC Sections 498A, 304B)

---------------------------------------------------

1. What is Dowry Harassment?
   Any demand for money, gifts, property, or valuables made before, during, or after marriage is illegal.  
   Dowry harassment includes:
      • Pressure for money or gifts  
      • Mental or physical cruelty  
      • Threats  
      • Violence related to dowry  
      • Coercion from husband or in-laws  

2. Where to File a Complaint
   A dowry complaint can be filed at:
      • Nearest Police Station  
      • Women Police Station / Mahila Thana  
      • Crime Against Women Cell (CAW Cell)  
      • National Commission for Women (NCW)  
      • District Legal Services Authority (Free legal aid)  
      • Online: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in (for digital threats)  

3. Relevant Laws for Dowry Cases
   • IPC 498A – Cruelty by husband or relatives  
   • IPC 304B – Dowry death  
   • Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961  
   • Domestic Violence Act, 2005 (for protection orders)  

4. How to File a Dowry Complaint (Step-by-Step)

   Step 1: Write a complaint describing:
      • Nature of harassment  
      • Dates of incidents  
      • People involved  
      • Evidence available (photos, chats, witnesses, medical reports)

   Step 2: Submit complaint to:
      • Police Station OR  
      • Women Help Desk OR  
      • Crime Against Women Cell (CAW/ Mahila Cell)

   Step 3: Police Actions:
      • Complaint registered  
      • FIR under IPC 498A & 406 (if applicable)  
      • Counseling or mediation (in CAW cell cases)  
      • Immediate protection if victim is unsafe  

   Step 4: FIR Registration (if crime confirmed)
      Police will register FIR under applicable sections and begin investigation.

5. What is CAW Cell Process?
   CAW Cell (Mahila Cell) handles complaints related to:
      • Dowry harassment  
      • Domestic violence  
      • Cruelty by in-laws  

   Steps:
      1. Written complaint submitted  
      2. Inquiry officer assigned  
      3. Counseling sessions arranged  
      4. If harassment proven → FIR recommended to police  

6. Evidence That Helps (Optional but Useful)
   • WhatsApp chats or call recordings  
   • Threat messages  
   • Photographs of injuries  
   • Medical reports  
   • Bank transfer proofs  
   • Witness statements  
   • Marriage-related financial demands  

7. FIR for Dowry Harassment (498A)
   • Cognizable & non-bailable offence  
   • Police MUST register FIR if cruelty/dowry harassment is established  
   • Arrest may not be immediate (Supreme Court guidelines), but protection is ensured  

8. Protection for the Victim
   Under Domestic Violence Act:
      • Protection Order  
      • Residence Order  
      • Monetary relief  
      • Custody orders  
      • Shelter home support  

9. Important Rights for Women
   • FIR can be filed in ANY police station (Zero FIR)  
   • Identity is protected in sensitive cases  
   • Police must record statement with dignity & sensitivity  
   • Legal aid is FREE for women  
   • No one can force the complaint to be withdrawn
