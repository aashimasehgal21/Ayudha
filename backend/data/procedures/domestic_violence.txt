Domestic Violence Procedure (Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

1. What qualifies as domestic violence?
   - Physical, emotional, sexual, verbal, or economic abuse
   - Threats or intimidation by husband or in-laws

2. Where to File a Complaint
   - Nearest Police Station
   - Protection Officer (PO) appointed under DV Act
   - Magistrate’s court (Direct complaint)
   - Women Helpline: 181
   - National Helpline: 1091

3. Filing a Domestic Incident Report (DIR)
   - Protection Officer prepares a DIR
   - Free of cost
   - Must be forwarded to Magistrate within 7 days

4. Reliefs Available
   - Protection Order (no violence)
   - Residence Order (right to stay in shared home)
   - Monetary relief (maintenance)
   - Custody orders for children
   - Compensation for emotional distress

5. Police Role
   - Immediate protection
   - Register FIR for cognizable offences (e.g., 498A, 354)
   - Assist Protection Officer

6. Court Process
   - Case hearing within 3 days
   - Final order within 60 days of first hearing

7. Shelter & Medical Support
   - Free shelter homes for victims
   - Government-funded medical aid
