Child Abuse Reporting Procedure (POCSO Act, 2012)
-------------------------------------------------

This procedure applies to reporting sexual offences against children under 
the Protection of Children from Sexual Offences (POCSO) Act, 2012.

1. Mandatory Reporting
   - ANY person who knows about child sexual abuse MUST report it.
   - Failure to report is a punishable offence under the Act.

2. Who Can File the Complaint
   - Child victim
   - Parent or guardian
   - Teacher, relative, neighbour, doctor
   - Any concerned citizen

3. Where to Report
   - Nearest police station (local or jurisdictional)
   - Special Juvenile Police Unit (SJPU)
   - Childline 1098 (emergency helpline)
   - District Child Protection Unit (DCPU)

4. FIR Registration
   - Police MUST register an FIR immediately.
   - Child need not visit the police station; officers must visit the child.
   - Female police officer should record the statement, preferably.

5. Child-Friendly Procedures
   - No child should be detained at the police station.
   - Police must ensure the child is not exposed to the accused.
   - Statements recorded at home or safe place, not necessarily police station.
   - Child can have parents/guardian present during statement.

6. Medical Examination
   - Must be conducted within 24 hours of reporting.
   - Preferably by a female doctor.
   - Consent from parent/guardian is required.

7. Support Person
   - Child Welfare Committee (CWC) assigns a "Support Person" to assist the child and family.
   - Support person helps with counselling, case updates, and legal procedures.

8. Recording Statement Before Magistrate
   - Magistrate records the child’s statement under Section 164 CrPC.
   - Child-friendly environment must be ensured.

9. Time-Bound Trial
   - Investigation must be completed within 2 months.
   - Trial must be completed within 1 year as far as possible.

10. Rehabilitation & Support
   - Free legal aid
   - Counselling and psychological care
   - Compensation under Victim Compensation Scheme
