FIR Registration Procedure (CrPC Section 154)
----------------------------------------------

1. What is an FIR?
   - FIR (First Information Report) is a written document prepared by the police when they receive information about a cognizable offence.
   - Police MUST register FIR for any cognizable crime, including violence, harassment, sexual offences, dowry, stalking, threats, or cyber abuse.

2. Where to File an FIR
   - Nearest Police Station
   - Women Police Station or Mahila Thana
   - Any police station (Zero FIR allowed)
   - Emergency Helpline: 112
   - Women Helpline: 1091
   - Cyber Crime: https://cybercrime.gov.in

3. What is Zero FIR?
   - A woman can file an FIR at ANY police station, even if the crime did not happen in that area.
   - The police must register the FIR and transfer it to the correct jurisdiction later.
   - Zero FIR is especially important for:
       • Sexual offences  
       • Domestic violence  
       • Kidnapping  
       • Assault  
       • Cyber harassment  

4. How to File an FIR
   Step 1: Visit any police station and explain the incident.
   Step 2: Police must record your statement in writing.
   Step 3: Survivor/victim must check the written FIR before signing.
   Step 4: FIR should include:
           • Date, time, place of incident  
           • Description of offence  
           • Details of accused (if known)  
           • Evidence or witnesses (optional)  
   Step 5: Police will register the FIR and give you:
           • Free copy of FIR  
           • FIR number  
           • Name and designation of the officer  

5. Your Rights During FIR
   - Police cannot refuse to register FIR for cognizable offences.
   - FIR can be filed by:
       • Victim  
       • Family member  
       • Friend  
       • Any witness  
   - Women have the right to:
       • Give statement to a lady officer  
       • Record statement at home/hospital (if injured)  
       • Identity confidentiality (for sexual offences)

6. FIR for Sexual Offences
   - FIR MUST be registered immediately (CrPC Sec 154(1)).
   - Statement recorded by woman police officer.
   - No delay allowed for jurisdiction reasons.
   - Child victims handled under POCSO child-friendly procedures.

7. Evidence to Carry (Optional)
   - Screenshots  
   - Photos/videos  
   - Injuries/medical records  
   - Chat logs  
   - Witness details  
   (FIR can also be filed even without evidence.)

8. Timeline After Filing FIR
   - Police begin investigation immediately.
   - Medical examination arranged (if required).
   - Statement before Magistrate recorded under Sec 164 CrPC.
   - Charge sheet usually within 60–90 days (depends on offence).

9. Important Points
   - FIR copy is free of cost.
   - No one can force you to withdraw an FIR.
   - Police cannot harass or threaten the complainant.
   - FIR cannot be rejected due to "less evidence" or "personal matter."
